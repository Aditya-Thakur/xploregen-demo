03Troubleshooting
Low DNA or RNA yield from an extraction kit

Check that lysis was complete, that the membrane or bead workflow was not overloaded, and that elution conditions match the sample type. Pre-warming elution buffer and allowing brief contact time before spin-out usually helps.

For lipid-rich or difficult matrices, lower input quantity and slightly longer lysis can improve reproducibility more than pushing maximum input mass.

A260/A280 ratio is below expectation

That usually points to carryover or a wash step issue. Confirm the blank was prepared with the same elution medium, and do not shortcut the protein wash or dry spin stages before elution.

If the sample class is especially protein-heavy, a second wash cycle is often more useful than increasing elution volume.

RNA prep shows DNA contamination downstream

Add an on-column DNase treatment if the downstream application is especially sensitive. Also review surface handling, pipette cleanliness, and thaw cycles if contamination appears inconsistently.

PCR purification recovery looks lower than expected

Short fragments often need extra attention. Isopropanol addition before loading, plus a smaller, warmed elution volume, usually improves recovery for low-length products.

Luciferase signal is weaker than expected

Make sure the reagent is fully equilibrated before use and avoid repeated freeze-thaw exposure. Signal variability often comes from handling conditions rather than assay chemistry alone.

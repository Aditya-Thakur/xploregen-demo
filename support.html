02Troubleshooting

Common issues, solved.

Low DNA/RNA yield from extraction kit

Check that the elution buffer was pre-warmed to 70 °C before use. Ensure the spin column membrane is completely dry before elution — an extra 2-minute dry spin at 13,000 × g helps. Also confirm that your input material was not over-homogenised, which can shear genomic DNA.

For tissues with high lipid content (liver, adipose), use up to 15 mg input instead of the standard 25 mg, and increase lysis time to 30 minutes.

A260/A280 ratio below 1.7

Low A260/A280 usually indicates protein carryover. Ensure the Protein Wash buffer step is not skipped. If the issue persists, increase the Protein Wash volume to 600 µL and do two wash cycles before the standard Wash Buffer step.

Also verify that your spectrophotometer blank was set using the same elution buffer or water used for elution.

RNA extraction showing DNA contamination in RT-PCR

Add an on-column DNase I digestion step (30 min at room temperature) after the first wash step. Our RNA kits include a DNase I digestion protocol in the CoA document. Alternatively, use 0.5 µL of RNase-free DNase (not included) per reaction in your RT step.

Ensure all work surfaces and pipettes are treated with RNaseZap or equivalent before starting.

PCR Purification Kit giving low recovery

For fragments below 200 bp, add 1 volume of isopropanol to the PCR reaction before loading onto the column. This improves binding efficiency for shorter fragments. Elute with 30 µL of pre-warmed (50 °C) elution buffer and allow the buffer to sit on the membrane for 2 minutes before spinning.

Luciferase signal lower than expected

Ensure the Firefly Bright Luciferase Reagent is fully equilibrated to room temperature (20–25 °C) before use. Signal is significantly reduced if added directly from –20 °C. Mix 1:1 volume of reagent with cell lysate and measure luminescence within 10 minutes for maximum signal. The reagent is sensitive to freeze-thaw cycling — aliquot into single-use volumes upon first thaw.

Gel smearing or multiple bands after DNA extraction

Smearing typically indicates incomplete digestion or RNA contamination. Ensure lysis is complete before adding the binding buffer. If RNA bands appear (~1.8 kb and ~4 kb), add 2 µL of RNase A (10 mg/mL) to the lysis step and incubate for 5 minutes at room temperature before proceeding.
